Interactive Experience Research Project

  1. What was the first thing you paid attention to when interacting with the experience?

    I find the bold color palette, art style and playful animation are very appealing. It gives a sense of fun and energetic, aligning with the their creative identity perfectly. I also think that smooth transitions and bouncy interactions are what attract users to the experience.

  2. Spend two minutes with the experience and create a list of each of your discrete actions.

    In two minutes, my main actions were clicking, scrolling, and choosing different activities. I played basketball, football, and boxing, ect then moved between sections quickly. Each action felt smooth and well-integrated, with minimal friction. My interaction was experimental yet fluid: click, observe, and move on. This shows the experience is designed for quick, fun and easy interactions without requiring long focus or deep navigation.

  3. What part of the experience did you spend the most time engaging with?

    I spent the most time interacting with the animated items, as they invited me to test and play. I personally think the responsiveness of these elements made the experience more engaging. I enjoyed the sense of control and discovery in seeing how each item reacted, which demonstrates the effectiveness of motion-driven design in holding user attention.

  4. What was the most common action in your two minute interaction with the experience?

    My most common actions were clicking and scrolling. The interaction design is built around these simple gestures, which makes it intuitive and easy to navigate. Every click felt rewarding because it triggered a fun response, while scrolling revealed new surprises, even though the transition was slightly slow in my opinion. This simplicity reinforces exploration while staying low effort. I personally liked it because it did not make me think, and seemed like developers had taken user actions and feedback considerably.

  5. What is your impression of the intended primary goal of the interactive experience?

    The primary goal is to provide fun, lighthearted engagement that showcases creativity through playful interaction. Rather than focusing on efficiency or delivering information, it invites users into an imaginative digital playground where curiosity drives exploration. Functionally, its purpose is to create a memorable impression through the integration of interaction, animation, and design.

  6. How does the interactive experience communicate this primary goal?

    The interactive experience communicates its goal through its playful visual language. Bright colors, bold shapes, and lively animations instantly signal that this is about fun and creativity. It feels like a digital playground, encouraging users to experiment freely. The seamless flow between sections also supports this, making it feel like one continuous, entertaining environment.

  7. What is your impression of how the experience should be interacted with over time? (For how long and how many different times)

    The experience feels designed for short, repeatable sessions rather than long, sustained use. Each visit can be quick and casual, yet still rewarding, which encourages replay and discovery of new elements. However, the movement and transition between sections could be faster, as sometimes the slower pace disrupts the playful rhythm and the flow of thoughts. Overall, it works best as a snackable experience rather than something users prefer to have quick access to main information.

  8. How does the interactive experience communicate how it should be interacted with over time?

    The design communicates a sense of openness and endlessness. There is not a fixed endpoint or pressure. It feels like you can stay as long as you want, or just dip in quickly. This freedom is supported by loop-like interactions, where animations and activities do not end but can be repeated infinitely. Therefore, I think the interactive experience has served its purpose as an invitation to return multiple times, rather than complete everything in one go.

  9. What other media forms (digital or otherwise) does the experience reference?

    The experience clearly references gaming, both in aesthetics and interaction style. The animations and playful mini-interactions reminded me of casual mobile games, where the focus is quick fun rather than heavy storytelling. The sporty elements, like basketball and boxing, also give it a gamified feeling. To me, these references make the design feel instantly familiar by borrowing the accessibility of gaming to create a lighthearted digital playground. It has a clever use of cultural cues that guide behavior intuitively.

  10. What does this reference/s communicate to you about how you should act when engaging with your research experience?

    The gaming reference communicates that mistakes do not exist here, only opportunities to explore. I felt relaxed and encouraged to approach the experience with curiosity and openness, as if I were playing a casual game. It suggested that the best way to engage is not passively but interactively, taking action and seeking surprises. This design choice effectively sets a tone of freedom and creativity, in my opinion.

  11. What does this reference/s communicate to you about how you should feel when engaging with your research experience?

    When engaging with my research experience, I should have fun rather than approach it seriously. It creates a light, joyful mood, similar to how one feels when opening a casual game app. The designs emotional intent was successful. I think users should feel amused, curious, and engaged while browsing rather than feeling stressed or pressured. To me, this emotional design choice makes the experience memorable.

  12. What is the most frustrating part of the interaction to you and what makes that part frustrating?

    The most frustrating part was the long loading time, which disrupted thinking flow. Another frustration was that Luni Life lacked interactivity compared to other sections, creating a sense of imbalance. I also felt slightly impatient, as the design had set me up to expect constant action and responsiveness. The interaction design would benefit from reducing wait times.

  13. What is the most satisfying part of the interaction to you and what makes that part satisfying?

    The most satisfying part was interacting with the animated items and seeing immediate feedback. Each click or scroll felt rewarding because it triggered a fun, lively response. The whole experience feels alive. I have learned from this strong interaction design: the feedback loop between action and response should be fast, intuitive, and enjoyable.
